Important — medication & diet rules
4 things to always
keep in mind.
1 — Never eat on an empty stomach
Some of your medications can be hard on the stomach lining when taken without food. Always have something to eat before any tablet — never on an empty stomach.
Even a small banana or 2 biscuits counts. Don't skip this.
2 — Tea & coffee: minimum 1 hour gap from meals
Tea and coffee can reduce absorption of certain nutrients when taken too close to meals. Keep at least 1 hour gap after eating before having chai or coffee.
This one simple habit makes a real difference to how well your body absorbs what you eat.
3 — Sundays: keep meals light and simple
Sunday is your rest day — keep meals gentle and easy on digestion. Khichdi, plain dal-roti, soft foods. Avoid anything too heavy, oily, or spicy on Sundays specifically.
Simple meals on this day help your body focus on recovery rather than digestion.
4 — Keep oil low — no fried food
Keep cooking oil to a minimum — max 2 tsp total per day across all meals. No deep fried snacks, no namkeen, no heavily oiled sabzi. Light tadka is perfectly fine.
You're already a non-drinker — that's a great advantage and makes a real positive difference to your health.
Sunlight: 15–20 min of morning sunlight daily, especially on arms and legs. Free, powerful, and one of the best things you can do for your joints and energy.

Anti-inflammatory foods — include regularly
Eat these as often as possible
· Haldi (turmeric) — add to warm milk, dal, sabzi daily. Most powerful anti-inflammatory spice available
· Adrak (ginger) — in chai (limited), cooking, or warm water in morning
· Jeera (cumin) — daily in tadka, khichdi, water
· Methi seeds — soak overnight, have 1 tsp in morning with water
· Palak (spinach) — iron + anti-inflammatory. In dal, sabzi, roti dough
· Moong dal — easiest to digest, lowest inflammation load of all dals
· Ghee (1 tsp/meal) — small amounts of ghee are anti-inflammatory and support joints
· Almonds (soaked) — 4–5 daily. Easier on the body when soaked and peeled
Haldi milk before bed is one of the simplest additions you can make — warm milk + ½ tsp turmeric + pinch of black pepper. Black pepper increases turmeric absorption by 20×.

Daily Walking — Every day including rest days
Start with 10–15 minutes daily. Build to 20–30 minutes over the month. Morning preferred. Any pace is fine — this is not cardio, it's recovery movement. Even walking around the house counts on bad days.
Walking is one of the best activities for Ankylosing Spondylitis — it keeps the spine mobile and reduces stiffness. Your doctor also recommended it.
Sleep — 7–8 hours minimum
You mentioned poor sleep quality. This is directly linked to inflammation — poor sleep raises inflammatory markers. Some things that help:

· Same sleep and wake time every day
· Haldi milk 30 min before bed (warm milk + turmeric + black pepper)
· No screens 30 min before sleeping
· Light dinner at least 2 hours before bed
With active joint inflammation, sleep quality is one of the highest-leverage things you can improve. It's not just rest — it's when your body repairs itself.
